About Marisela Meskus

Career Transition (2020 – 2022)

I am pursuing a Certificate in User Experience and Web Design from the UCSC Silicon Valley Extension (Winter 2021 – Winter 2022).

I first became interested in UX Design after getting the chance to create some webpages for the Cushing J. Library at Holy Names University. As the Access Services Supervisor, I was only responsible for updating the webpages assigned to me. But during the library building closure, I recognized a need. I suggested and designed new webpages that provided library service updates and community resources for students adjusting the learning online during the pandemic.

Supporting End-users (2017 – 2020)

My work in libraries started in 2017 when I volunteered as a research assistant at the Oakland FamilySearch Library and at the California Genealogical Society library (also located in Oakland, CA) as part of the research team.

These experiences enabled me to get hired as a library assistant at the historic Mechanics' Institute in San Francisco. Within 6 months, I worked my way up from an on-call to a part-time position. In 2018, I earned the full-time position of Serials & Public Services Library Assistant. I managed both departments until 2020.

Family Genealogy Research

I have been researching my family history since 2005, when my family took a trip to the Dominican Republic–my father's birthplace. I earned a Certificate in Genealogical Research from Boston University in 2017 and served on the board of the California Genealogical Society from 2018 to 2020.

Education

I received a Master of Arts in English (Linguistics) from San Francisco State University in 2015 and a Bachelor of Arts in History from UC Merced in 2011. I am a 1st generation college graduate who was born in Merced and transferred from Merced College.
